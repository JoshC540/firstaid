Techniques
Recovery Position
Illustrated step-by-step guide
Applies to 6 conditions

The recovery position keeps an unconscious but breathing person's airway open and prevents choking on vomit. It is used across multiple emergencies — any time a person is unconscious and breathing but you are waiting for 112.

Step-by-Step
1
Kneel beside them
Position yourself at their side. Straighten their legs.
Check they are breathing before proceeding. If not breathing — begin CPR.
2
Position the near arm
Place the arm closest to you at a right angle to their body, elbow bent, palm facing up.
This arm acts as a stop to prevent rolling too far onto the front.
3
Bring the far hand to their cheek
Take their far hand and place the back of it against the near cheek. Hold it there.
Keep holding their hand against their cheek with your hand to guide and protect the head as you roll them.
4
Bend the far knee up
With your other hand, pull the far knee up so the foot is flat on the ground.
This bent knee will act as the lever you use to roll them in the next step.
5
Roll them toward you
Pull the bent knee toward you to roll them onto their side. Guide the head with your other hand.
Use the bent knee as the lever — pull it toward you firmly. Keep one hand supporting the head throughout the roll.
6
Open the airway and monitor
Tilt the head back gently to open the airway. Adjust the hand position under the cheek. Check they are still breathing.
The top knee prevents rolling forward. The bottom arm prevents rolling back. Check breathing every 30 seconds and recheck until 112 arrives.
When to use the recovery position
Applies to these conditions in this app
Stroke Cardiac Arrest (post-ROSC) Anaphylaxis Seizures Head Injury Drowning
Do Not Use If
Do not place in the recovery position if a spinal injury is suspected — keep still and wait for emergency services. Do not use if the person is not breathing — begin CPR instead.
Infants under 12 months

Do not use the standard recovery position for infants. Instead, hold the infant face-down along your forearm, head slightly lower than the body, keeping the airway open. Support the head with your hand.

Key checks while waiting for 112
Check breathing every 30 seconds — look for chest rise, listen, feel for breath on your cheek.
If they stop breathing at any point — roll them onto their back and begin CPR immediately.
Do not leave them alone — stay with them until paramedics arrive.
If they must remain in the recovery position for more than 30 minutes, carefully roll them to the other side to relieve pressure on the lower arm.
